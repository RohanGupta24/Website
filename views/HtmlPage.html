Personal Details

My initial programming experience started when I took a game design course at DigiPen Instititue of Technology. I then took an Object-Oriented programming course at University of Washington before deciding to enroll in AP Computer Science in my high school.

I have gained a strong technical background over the summer when I interned at UPS as a software engineer. I helped develop a mobile and web interface that allows jobs to be dispatched to drivers automatically so that planes may be loaded and unloaded more frequently and efficiently. The following are some of the accomplishments and skills I have gained this summer at UPS: (1) I learned about different software platforms such as Visual Studio, Xamarin, SQL Server Management Studio, Couchbase, and OpenShift. (2) I programmed in different languages and frameworks such as HTML & CSS, Javascript (Angular JS and D3.js), C#, Python, SQL, and XAML. (3) I was introduced to different hardware platforms and devices that can be integrated with software such as Raspberry Pi, Arduino, and the Intel Recon Pro glasses. (4) I experienced first-hand the agile software development process, which included three-week iteration meetings, daily standup meetings, and pair programming; I learned how to make user stories and assign tasks on the Team Foundation Server (TFS). (5) I worked with a technical lead programmer and a business analyst to understand the development of the product and the business opportunities of our project; I helped test our app by receiving advice and feedback from potential users. (6) I gained strong communication skills through numerous technical presentations of our project and app. (7) I prepared a technical document outlining the project's goals and the specifics of our implementation. (8) Accomplishments: Our app is estimated to save drivers approximately 2 to 3 hours; the app is currently deployed to around 200 users; and the company has extended this app to include other areas of package transportation.

I have participated in several hackathons and programming-related events including Boilermake, HackIllinois, HackGT, and BigRedHacks. Some of the projects I have worked on include Prescription Merge Conflict, a simple web app that informs patients which combination of medications are considered dangerous, and Music Recommendation, an app that suggests songs and music based on Spotify user history.
